PATIENTS IN-HOUSE: This Stats Card displays the total number of patients that are undergoing treatment and have been admitted in the hospital. Everytime a patient approaches the doctor for treatment, it automatically increments the PATIENTS IN-HOUSE value by one to signify the total number of patients currently admitted in the hospital.
It displays the overall performance (here 14.52%) by comparing last month's patient intake with the present month's intake.


Available Staff
25

3.48% Since last week

AVAILABLE STAFF: This Stats Card displays the total number of staff members available in the hospital premises. If a new staff member is hired, then this value is incremented by 1.
It calculates the employment rate of the present month and compares it with employment rate of the previous month to determine the increase or decrease in rate of employment (here 3.48%).


Appointments
27

12.48% Since last month

APPOINTMENTS: As the name suggests, this Stats Card simply displays the upcoming appointments scheduled for the doctor this month. It fetches this information from the doctor's calendar so whenever a new appointment event is created, it increments this value by 1.
It displays the overall appointment booking rate (here 12.48%) by comparing the total appointments from last month with the present month.

RECOVERY RATE: This Stats Card is of immence importance as it displays the percentage of leads successfully converted out of all the leads that were in-progress in a week.
It displays the overall performance (here 23.67%) by comparing the total leads converted from last week with the present week.

Patients Treated (yearly)


PATIENTS TREATED CHART:
This chart plots the graph for the total number of patients treated for every month of the year. Additionally, it also plots the same graph for the previous year as well. This enables us to allocate resources and make strategies based on the patients treated output for each month.

TIP #1: Hover your mouse cursor over the graph to see and compare the datasets for each month between the two years.

TIP #2: Click on the 2018 or 2019 box (index key) in the chart to disable the datasets of a specific year so as to focus on just one graph.
